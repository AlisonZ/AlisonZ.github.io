I am a linguist turned Software Engineer who is transitioning back to linguistics. My initial work in linguistics research was focused on language documentation and revitalization. I had the opportunity to work with the Pure'pecha and Iquito communities on separate documentation projects. As a software engineer I have been working in the corporate space honing my skills as an engineer. 

I began the Master's Program in Computational Linguistics at University of Washington to return to my true passion and interest in languages. I am hoping to merge the new skills I have garnered in my engineering career to be able to assist me in language documentation projects. My goals and focus in my work is to help to enable communities in the preservation, discovery and revitalization of their languages. I am looking forward to using computational linguistics techniques and approaches to supplement this.

In addition and related to language revitalization work, I am deeply interested in the ethics and morality of using large language models and "AI". I hope to pursue work that is grounded in ethical use of these tools as well as protects the sovereignty of language communities. As these tools are being adopted in a widespread fashion, I am interested in how we can enact policies that keep us and our data safe.
